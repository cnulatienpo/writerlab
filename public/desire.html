🔥 Desire

Desire is what drives the character forward—even when they don’t admit it, even when they fight it. It’s the emotional gravity that shapes choice, conflict, and change.

📘 Lesson

Desire is the fuel of story. It’s not always about speaking up. Sometimes desire shows up in silence, in longing, in a terrible decision that says everything. It doesn’t have to be romantic. It doesn’t have to be dramatic. It just has to matter.

📚 Examples

Ratatouille – Remy wants to cook, but rats aren’t allowed in kitchens.
Moonlight – Chiron wants safety and intimacy, but hides it behind silence.
The Little Mermaid – Ariel wants the human world—and gives up her voice to reach it.

🎭 Tropes: Add Desire

The Forbidden Want – They want something they’re not supposed to want.
The Hidden Crush – Deep affection kept secret.
Object of Obsession – They can’t stop chasing it.
The Dream That Haunts – They had it once. They lost it.
The Deal with the Devil – They want it badly enough to break a rule.
In Their Dreams – Their desire leaks into nightmares or visions.
The Spark – They didn’t want anything... until now.
Chasing Glory – They want to be remembered.
Redemption Arc – They want to undo something they regret.
The Forbidden Door – They want to open what should stay closed.

🧊 Tropes: Soften Desire

Polite Deflection – They downplay what they want.
Resigned Acceptance – They’ve given up wanting.
Dream Deferred – They set it aside… for now.
It’s Not About Me – They pretend they’re doing it for someone else.
The Mask – They hide what they want behind a role.
The Noble Sacrifice – They give up their want for someone else’s sake.
The False Front – They act like they want something else entirely.
Letting Go – They actively stop chasing it.
The Quiet Want – They never say it. But we feel it.
Muted Fire – Their want burns low and slow, never spoken.
