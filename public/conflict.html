⚔️ Conflict

Conflict is what makes a story feel like something's happening. It's tension, friction, resistance—someone wants something, and something's in the way.

📘 Lesson

Conflict can be loud or quiet, external or internal, explosive or subtle. It can come from another person, a rule, a system, a feeling, a belief. What matters is that something resists. And that resistance forces characters to react, change, or break.

📚 Examples

Pride and Prejudice – Elizabeth wants to stay independent. Darcy wants her to see he’s changed. The conflict is emotional, internal, and social.
The Hunger Games – Katniss wants to survive and protect her sister. The system wants her to die for entertainment.
The Office – Michael wants Toby gone. Toby just wants to exist. Conflict is loud, petty, personal.

🔥 Tropes: Add Conflict

The Betrayal – Someone breaks trust.
The Secret – One person knows something, the other doesn’t.
Race Against the Clock – Time is running out.
The Ultimatum – "Do this, or lose everything."
The Rival – Two people want the same thing.
Moral Dilemma – No right answer, only consequences.
Person vs. Self – They’re their own worst enemy.
One Lie Too Many – The truth finally breaks through.
The Wrong Time – They finally say it—too late.
Locked Door – The path is literally or figuratively blocked.
The Interruption – The moment is ruined by something unplanned.
Love vs Duty – Their heart wants one thing, their role demands another.
The Misunderstanding – They're not on the same page.
The Trap – They walk right into it.
The Unexpected Visitor – A complication arrives mid-scene.
The Power Grab – Someone makes a move to take control.
The Rebellion – Someone refuses to follow the rules.
The Blame Game – Everyone's pointing fingers.
Running Out of Options – Every door is closing.
The Threat – Something bad is promised if they don't comply.

🧊 Tropes: Reduce Conflict

Silent Standoff – No one speaks. Tension simmers.
Polite Denial – Everyone pretends nothing’s wrong.
Mutual Distraction – Focus shifts away from the problem.
Shared Grief – Pain softens the edges.
Strategic Retreat – They back off—for now.
Change the Subject – One of them shifts the conversation.
Peace Offering – They offer something small to ease tension.
Fake Agreement – They nod, but they’re not on board.
The Apology – Even if it’s not sincere, it cools things down.
The Hug – A physical gesture dissolves the tension.
Letting It Slide – They choose not to fight.
Comic Relief – A joke breaks the tension.
The Outsider Interrupts – A third party cuts the tension.
Agree to Disagree – They set it aside.
Time Skip – The story moves ahead before it boils.
Busy With Survival – They’re too focused on danger to argue.
Bonding Moment – Something good slips through the tension.
One-Sided Exit – One of them walks away instead.
Resigned Acceptance – They give up the fight.
The Quiet After – The tension passed. Nobody mentions it.
